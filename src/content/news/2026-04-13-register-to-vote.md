## How to register to vote  
  
[Register to vote](https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote)

([https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote](https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote))

To register to vote online, you need just five minutes and your National Insurance number.

If you can’t register online, you can ++[download a paper form++](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/register-to-vote-paper-forms) 

([https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/register-to-vote-paper-forms](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/register-to-vote-paper-forms).)

You don’t need to register to vote before every election.

You must register to vote at the address you live at. If you live at two addresses because you are a ++[student or have a second home](https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/voting-and-elections/who-can-vote/other-registration-options/voting-and-second-homes)++, you may be able to register to vote at both addresses.

([https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/voting-and-elections/who-can-vote/other-registration-options/voting-and-second-homes](https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/voting-and-elections/who-can-vote/other-registration-options/voting-and-second-homes))

### Update your details

If you move house, you should register to vote again.

If you change your name for any reason, you can either:

- ++[contact the electoral services team](https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/voting-and-elections/register-vote#check-if-registered-en)++ at your local council and request a change of name form
- register to vote again ([https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/voting-and-elections/register-vote#check-if-registered-en](https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk/voting-and-elections/register-vote#check-if-registered-en))
